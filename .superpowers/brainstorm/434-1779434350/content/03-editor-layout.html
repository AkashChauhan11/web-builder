A

Elementor-style (left panel swaps)

Pro: truly feels like Elementor. One focused panel. More canvas width. Modern.
Con: bigger rewrite of the current GrapesJS UI (we'd hide the right panel and build a custom left-side widget-settings UI). +1-2 weeks of work.

B

GrapesJS-style (left blocks + right styles)

Pro: works with how GrapesJS is built. Less custom UI. Faster to ship Phase A. We just heavily restyle the right panel to Elementor quality.
Con: doesn't feel exactly like Elementor. Two panels eat canvas width. (Mitigation: panels collapse with hotkeys.)

C

Hybrid — left swaps, right collapsible

Left panel swaps between Widgets/Navigator/Global like Elementor. Right panel is hidden by default but can slide out for advanced controls (custom CSS, deep settings). Best of both, more work than B but less than A.

My recommendation: Option B for Phase A — it lets us ship the substance (sections, widgets, style panel) faster. We can revisit the layout in Phase B/C if it doesn't feel right. Phase A success = it has the features and the right panel looks polished, even if the geometry isn't pure Elementor.
